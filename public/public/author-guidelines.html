Author guidelines for Hature

The sections below provide essential information for authors and contributors wishing to submit to Hature. These instructions apply to research articles, reviews, perspectives and general studies essays, and are intended to keep the submission process clear, lightweight and internationally accessible.

Aims and scope

Hature is a mock academic journal platform centered on Hachimi studies while remaining open to broader interdisciplinary discussion. The journal welcomes original, clearly argued and well-structured submissions across four core sections.

哈基米信息学

Research on the underlying code, logical structure, symbolic systems and formal modelling of Hachimi.

哈基米行为学

Research on observable Hachimi behaviour, reactions, interaction patterns and context-dependent performance.

基米社会学

Research on social relations, collective structures, networks and interactional meanings related to Hachimi worlds.

哈基米综合学

Open interdisciplinary work related or unrelated to Hachimi, provided that the argument is clear, substantial and coherent.

Hature values clarity, originality, structure and readability more than adherence to a single disciplinary style.

Article types

Type Typical length Description
Research Article 3,000–8,000 words Full original study with a clear question, method or analytical structure, results or argument, and conclusion.
Review 4,000–10,000 words Synthesis of an existing topic, concept or field relevant to Hature’s sections.
Perspective 1,500–4,000 words Argument-driven piece presenting a position, conceptual intervention or methodological viewpoint.
Methods / Framework 2,000–6,000 words Description of a framework, model, annotation scheme, analytical pipeline or editorial method.
General Studies Essay 1,500–6,000 words Cross-disciplinary or boundary-expanding work suitable for Hachimi General Studies.

Editorial criteria and process

Submissions will normally be assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

  • Clear fit with one of Hature’s sections or a persuasive justification for cross-sectional relevance.
  • Originality of question, argument, model, method or interpretation.
  • Logical structure, internal consistency and readability.
  • Appropriate use of evidence, examples, references or formal reasoning.
  • Reasonable presentation quality and respect for journal policies.

The editorial process typically includes initial screening, format check, editorial assessment and, where appropriate, external review or internal commentary. Hature reserves the right to return submissions for technical revision before editorial consideration.

Formatting guide

To keep submissions simple and accessible, Hature accepts a flexible but standard manuscript structure.

Required element Guideline
Title Concise and informative. Preferably under 25 words.
Author information Full names, affiliations and corresponding author email.
Abstract 150–300 words, summarizing the question, approach and main conclusion.
Keywords 3–8 keywords.
Main text Recommended headings: Introduction, Main analysis / Results, Discussion, Conclusion.
References Any consistent academic style is acceptable at initial submission. Consistency matters more than format preference.
Figures and tables Provide numbered titles and brief legends. Cite each in the text.
At initial submission, Hature does not require strict adherence to a single citation style. Clean structure and consistency are sufficient.

Language policy

Hature accepts submissions in multiple languages. English is recommended for widest accessibility, but manuscripts in Chinese and other languages may also be considered if the writing is clear and appropriately structured.

Authors may submit bilingual titles, abstracts or keywords where useful. If a manuscript is not in English, we strongly recommend including an English title and abstract whenever possible.

Language polishing, including AI-assisted language refinement, is permitted, provided that the scientific or intellectual responsibility remains entirely with the authors.

Initial submission

At initial submission, authors should provide the following:

  • Main manuscript file in PDF, DOCX or compatible format.
  • Title, author information, abstract and keywords.
  • Section selection corresponding to the journal’s four core categories.
  • Any figures, tables or supplementary files that are directly relevant to the submission.
  • A brief data and code availability statement where applicable.

Hature encourages authors to prioritize clarity and completeness over strict stylistic polish at the first submission stage.

Final submission

If a manuscript is accepted or invited for revision, authors may be asked to provide a clean final version, editable source files, higher-quality figures and updated metadata.

Final files should include all corrections requested by the editorial team, consistent references, properly numbered figures and tables, and any final declarations required for publication.

Supplementary information

Supplementary information may include appendices, additional figures, datasets, code files, extended methods, annotation materials or supporting documentation that are directly relevant to the submission.

  • Accepted formats may include PDF, ZIP, TXT, CSV, XLSX, PNG, JPG and common code or text formats.
  • Each supplementary item should be clearly named and briefly described.
  • Supplementary materials should be cited in the main text where relevant.

Ethics, originality and AI use

Submissions to Hature must be original, appropriately attributed and not misleading in authorship or source. Authors are responsible for the accuracy of their text, data, claims, citations and representations.

Use of AI tools is allowed under the following conditions:

  • AI-assisted language polishing, restructuring, translation and drafting support are permitted.
  • Authors remain fully responsible for all submitted content.
  • AI tools should not be listed as authors.
  • If AI was used in a substantial way for writing, image generation or data-assisted analysis, authors should disclose this in an acknowledgement or methods note where appropriate.

Hature does not prohibit AI polishing. It requires transparency, accountability and human responsibility.

Fees and access

Hature currently does not charge submission fees, review fees or article processing charges. In particular, there are no page charges or mandatory publication fees at the present stage.

Authors should note that this policy may be updated in the future if the journal platform changes, but any such change would be clearly announced in advance.

Forms and declarations

Depending on the submission type, authors may be asked to provide the following declarations:

  • Corresponding author confirmation
  • Originality statement
  • Conflict of interest statement
  • Data availability statement
  • AI use disclosure, where relevant

At this stage, Hature keeps declarations lightweight and focused on transparency rather than administrative burden.
